- The CCI was developed by Donald Lambert in 1980 as a way to identify cyclical turns in commodities.
- It's calculated as (Typical Price - Simple Moving Average of Typical Price) / (0.015 x Mean Absolute Deviation).
- Typical Price = (High + Low + Close)/3
- The CCI oscillates around a central line of 0, with readings above +100 considered overbought and below -100 considered oversold.

- CCI Divergence - Identify bullish or bearish divergences between the CCI and the price action to spot potential trend changes.
- CCI Crossovers - Use CCI crossovers of the 0 line as buy/sell signals, entering long when the CCI crosses above 0 and short when it crosses below.
- CCI Overbought/Oversold - Look for the CCI to move above +100 (overbought) or below -100 (oversold) as potential reversal signals.
- CCI Trend Following - Use the CCI to confirm the direction of the trend, going long when the CCI is positive and short when it's negative.

3. Advantages and Disadvantages of CCI for Crypto Trading:
Advantages:

- Can provide early signals of trend changes
- Works well in ranging and trending markets

Disadvantages:
