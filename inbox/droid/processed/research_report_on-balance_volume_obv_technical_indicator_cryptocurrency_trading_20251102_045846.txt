The OBV calculation is based on the relationship between the current day's closing price and the previous day's closing price:
1. If the current day's closing price is greater than the previous day's closing price, the current day's volume is added to the running OBV total.
2. If the current day's closing price is less than the previous day's closing price, the current day's volume is subtracted from the running OBV total.
3. If the current day's closing price is equal to the previous day's closing price, the OBV total remains unchanged.

The formula for calculating OBV is:
OBV = Previous OBV + Current Volume
   When Close > Previous Close, then Current Volume is added.
   When Close < Previous Close, then Current Volume is subtracted.
   When Close = Previous Close, OBV is unchanged.

Interpreting OBV:
1. Positive OBV Trend: When the OBV line is trending upward, it indicates that volume is flowing into the stock, which is a bullish sign. This confirms the price trend.
2. Negative OBV Trend: When the OBV line is trending downward, it indicates that volume is flowing out of the stock, which is a bearish sign. This confirms the price trend.
3. Divergence: When the price of the stock is making new highs or lows, but the OBV is not making new highs or lows, it is called a divergence. This can be a sign of an impending trend reversal.
