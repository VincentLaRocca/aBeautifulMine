Answer: Here is a detailed overview of the "bitcoin median transaction fee" and its effect on the development of Layer-2 protocols:

Bitcoin Median Transaction Fee:
- The bitcoin median transaction fee is the middle value of all the fees paid by users to have their transactions included in the bitcoin blockchain.
- This fee fluctuates based on network demand and congestion. When the bitcoin network is busy, the median fee tends to rise as users compete to have their transactions processed faster.
- According to data from BitInfoCharts, the bitcoin median transaction fee has ranged from as low as $0.05 to as high as $62.78 over the past 5 years. The current median fee is around $1.50.
- High bitcoin transaction fees can make small value transactions uneconomical, as the fee may exceed the value being transferred.

Effect on Layer-2 Protocols:
- The fluctuating and sometimes high bitcoin transaction fees have been a key driver behind the development of Layer-2 protocols like the Lightning Network.
- Layer-2 protocols are built on top of the base bitcoin blockchain to enable faster, cheaper, and more scalable transactions without congesting the main network.
- The Lightning Network allows users to open payment channels and conduct transactions off-chain, only settling on the bitcoin blockchain periodically. This dramatically reduces the fees required for each individual transaction.
- Other Layer-2 protocols like the Liquid Network also aim to provide faster, cheaper transactions by moving some activity off the main bitcoin chain.
- The growth of these Layer-2 networks has been strongly correlated with periods of high bitcoin network congestion and fees, as users seek alternative solutions.
- Experts believe that the continued development of robust Layer-2 protocols will be crucial for bitcoin to scale and handle mainstream adoption, without users being priced out by high transaction costs.
