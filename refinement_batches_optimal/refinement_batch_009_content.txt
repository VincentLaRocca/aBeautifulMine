   - In a congested network, users may need to pay higher transaction fees to ensure their transactions are processed in a timely manner.
   - Miners typically prioritize transactions with higher fees, leading to a competitive fee market during periods of high network activity.

   - Prolonged network congestion can put strain on the underlying infrastructure, potentially leading to network instability, delays, or even temporary outages.
