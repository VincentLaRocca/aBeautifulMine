Question: Challenges and limitations of using active addresses monthly metric
Answer: Certainly! Here's a detailed overview of the challenges and limitations of using active addresses monthly as a metric:

1. Metric Definition and Interpretation:
   - Active addresses monthly refers to the number of unique addresses that have been involved in a transaction on the blockchain during a given month.
   - This metric is often used as a proxy for user activity and adoption of a cryptocurrency or blockchain network.
   - However, the interpretation of this metric can be complex and may not provide a complete picture of the network's health and usage.

2. Limitations:
   - Address Reuse: Individuals or entities may use multiple addresses for their transactions, leading to an overestimation of the number of unique users.
   - Inactive Addresses: Addresses that have been used in the past but are currently dormant are still counted as active, even though they may not represent ongoing user activity.
   - Automated Transactions: Some addresses may be associated with automated processes, such as exchanges, mining pools, or smart contracts, which can skew the active addresses metric.
   - Wallet Consolidation: Users may consolidate their funds into fewer addresses over time, leading to a decrease in the active addresses metric, even if the actual user activity remains the same or increases.
   - Lack of Context: The active addresses metric alone does not provide information about the frequency, volume, or nature of the transactions, which can be crucial for understanding the network's usage and adoption.

3. Challenges:
   - Distinguishing Unique Users: It is challenging to accurately identify and count the number of unique users on a blockchain network, as individuals may use multiple addresses or wallets.
   - Differentiating Human and Automated Activity: Separating human-initiated transactions from automated or machine-generated transactions can be complex, leading to potential misinterpretation of the active addresses metric.
   - Accounting for Wallet Behavior Changes: As user wallets and transaction patterns evolve over time, the active addresses metric may not accurately reflect the changes in user behavior and network usage.
   - Comparing across Blockchains: Directly comparing active addresses metrics across different blockchain networks can be problematic due to differences in network architecture, user behavior, and transaction patterns.

4. Complementary Metrics:
   - To gain a more comprehensive understanding of a blockchain network's usage and adoption, it is recommended to consider a combination of metrics, such as:
     - Transaction volume and value
     - Number of new addresses created
     - Address balance distribution
     - Network activity and on-chain analytics
     - User engagement and retention metrics
   - These complementary metrics can provide a more nuanced and accurate assessment of the network's health and growth.
