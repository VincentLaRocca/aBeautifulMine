The Wedge Pattern:
A wedge pattern is a technical analysis chart pattern that is formed by two converging trend lines. There are two types of wedge patterns:

1. Rising Wedge:
- A rising wedge is formed by two ascending trend lines that converge as prices move higher.
- The upper trend line connects the highs, while the lower trend line connects the lows.
- Rising wedges are generally considered a bearish reversal pattern, as they often signal the end of an uptrend.

2. Falling Wedge:
- A falling wedge is formed by two descending trend lines that converge as prices move lower. 
- The upper trend line connects the highs, while the lower trend line connects the lows.
- Falling wedges are generally considered a bullish reversal pattern, as they often signal the end of a downtrend.

Key Characteristics:
- Converging Trend Lines: The two trend lines that form the wedge pattern must be converging, meaning they are getting closer together over time.
- Decreasing Volume: Volume tends to decrease as the wedge pattern forms, reflecting diminishing momentum.
- Breakout Direction: The direction of the breakout from the wedge pattern (upwards or downwards) is considered the likely future trend direction.

Identification and Analysis:
- To identify a wedge pattern, look for two converging trend lines that connect a series of higher highs and higher lows (rising wedge) or lower highs and lower lows (falling wedge).
- Analyze the volume profile - decreasing volume as the pattern forms is a key characteristic.
- The breakout from the wedge, whether upwards or downwards, is an important signal for future price direction.
